What does CBD stand for?

Cannabis plants have around 60 unique compounds that aren’t found in any other plants. Of these compounds, THC and CBD are present in the highest concentrations. CBD stands for Cannabidiol and this compound has garnered immense interest in the scientific community. It offers several therapeutic benefits and can be used to aid in the relief of a wide range of common illnesses and natural conditions that we experience throughout our lives. That’s one of the reasons why governments are sponsoring research into this compound.

What is the difference between CBD and THC?

THC stands for Tetrahydrocannabinol and it is one of the most dominant compounds in cannabis. Every strain has a different ratio of THC/CBD, and that determines how the strain affects a person. THC-rich strains will cause a high because it is psychoactive. CBD is non-psychoactive; the ingredient actively counteracts THC’s high-inducing properties.

This compound is an antipsychotic, effective for people looking to aid in the relief of things like paranoia or anxiety. CBD has no affinity with CB1 receptors in the brain while THC has a strong affinity with them. These pathways manage psychoactive effects and THC triggers them. While CBD takes a different pathway, it provides similar health benefits as THC, and so CBD is considered a safer alternative to THC.
